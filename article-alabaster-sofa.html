Meet the Alabaster Sofa

10 Apr 2025 8 min read By Winteriors
Introduction

Some furniture pieces quietly demand attention — not through loudness, but through confidence. The Alabaster Sofa is exactly that. A low-profile, contemporary 3-seater finished in a soft cream fabric, it carries the kind of effortless elegance that makes a room feel considered and complete. We built it for people who want their living space to feel like a refuge — unhurried, refined, and entirely their own.

This is not a sofa that shouts. It whispers — through the clean straight silhouette, the slender black metal legs, the generous bench seat, and the two plush scatter cushions that invite you to sink in and stay a while. Whether anchoring a minimalist apartment in Lusaka's Kabulonga or gracing the lobby of a boutique hotel, the Alabaster sofa earns its place without trying.

Care & Maintenance

Keeping It Beautiful

Light-coloured fabric upholstery rewards a little regular attention. The good news is that none of it is complicated — a consistent routine is all it takes to keep the Alabaster looking fresh for years.

Dusting

Use a soft-bristle upholstery brush or the brush attachment of a vacuum cleaner once a week to lift dust, pet hair, and fine particles from the fabric surface and seat crevices. Always brush in the direction of the weave.

Wiping & Spot Cleaning

For fresh spills, blot immediately with a clean dry cloth — never rub. For light stains, use a barely damp cloth with a small amount of mild fabric detergent. Work from the outside of the stain inward and allow to air dry fully.

Deep Cleaning

Every 3–4 months, use an upholstery foam cleaner or a fabric steamer for a deeper clean. Allow the sofa to dry completely in a well-ventilated room before use. Avoid soaking the fabric or using harsh chemical cleaners.

Deodorising

Lightly sprinkle baking soda over the fabric, leave for 20–30 minutes, then vacuum thoroughly. This neutralises odours naturally without chemical residue. Repeat monthly or as needed, especially in humid conditions.

Scenting

Avoid spraying perfume or air freshener directly on the fabric. Instead, place a linen sachet with dried lavender or cedar inside a cushion cover, or use a fabric-safe linen spray misted lightly from at least 30 cm away.

Leg & Frame Care

Wipe the black powder-coated metal legs with a barely damp cloth to remove dust. Dry immediately to prevent water marks. Check leg screws every 6 months and tighten if needed to keep the frame firm and stable.

Placement

Where It Works Best

The Alabaster's neutral cream tone and restrained profile make it one of the most versatile sofas we carry. Below are the settings where it performs best — and why.

The Modern Living Room

Place the Alabaster facing a low media console or a gallery wall of bold art — much like the photograph above. Add a textured jute rug underneath to ground it, a small white or wooden side table to the right, and a floor lamp to the left. The cream tone reflects natural light beautifully, making the space feel larger and airier than it is.

Hotel & Guest Lounge

In a hotel lobby or guest lounge, the Alabaster reads as premium without being cold. Pair two units facing each other across a low marble or glass coffee table, flanked by armchairs in a contrasting fabric. The low seat height keeps sightlines open across the room — ideal for spaces that need to feel both welcoming and uncluttered.

Executive Office Reception

Position the sofa against a feature wall — ideally a dark painted or panelled surface that contrasts with the cream — to create a reception seating area that makes visitors feel at ease from the moment they walk in. A single potted plant and a low side table complete the look without overcrowding the space.

Open-Plan Apartment

In an open-plan layout, use the Alabaster to define the living zone within a larger room. Position it with its back to the dining area, creating a natural boundary. A large area rug underneath reinforces the separation. The sofa's low profile ensures it doesn't visually chop the room in half.

Interior Decor

Styling Suggestions

The Alabaster is a neutral anchor — it works with a wide range of palettes and styles. Here are four popular setups that suit most Zambian homes and offices.

Palette Warm Neutrals

Pair with terracotta cushions, a warm-toned wooden coffee table, and a sisal rug. Wall colour: warm white or soft sand. This is the most popular combination for residential living rooms — easy, cohesive, and timeless.

Palette Monochrome Contrast

Keep the sofa cream, add black scatter cushions, a black metal coffee table, and a charcoal grey rug. A single large black-framed artwork above completes the look. Suits minimalist apartments and executive offices equally well.

Palette Nature & Green

Introduce sage green or forest green through cushion covers, a trailing potted plant, and linen curtains in a muted olive tone. The cream sofa becomes the calm centre between organic textures — ideal for homes that lean into biophilic design.

Palette Bold Art, Quiet Base

Let the sofa be the quiet base for a bold art-forward room. A large, colourful abstract painting above — as seen in our product photo — does all the expressive work while the cream sofa keeps the room grounded and liveable.
